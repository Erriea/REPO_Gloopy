# High Concept Document

## Title
Gloopy Goes Home

## Genre
Stylized top-down traversal / body-hopping puzzle game made in Unity.

## Core Loop
The player controls Gloopy, a stranded alien parasite that cannot move in its base form. To travel, the player must click nearby creatures, infect them, and temporarily control them. Each host has a limited lifespan, so the player must keep switching bodies to continue moving across land, water, and obstacle-filled spaces until they reach the spaceship and escape.

## Visual Style
The game uses a playful, readable presentation inspired by 2D-character-in-3D-world games such as *Don't Starve*. The world is built from simple stylized geometry and custom shaders for surfaces like water, grass, and rock. The tone is weird and light rather than graphic or horror-focused.

## Intended Experience
The intended experience is a mix of tension, improvisation, and route planning. The player should feel clever when chaining together the right host types, anxious when stranded in base form, and rewarded for reading the map and adapting quickly to changing situations. The game should feel strange, readable, and playful from start to finish.

## Game Summary
- Walkers move on land and are blocked by water and obstacles.
- Swimmers move through water but are dangerous if their timer expires while still in the water.
- Flyers can cross land and water while floating above obstacles.
- Police only threaten Gloopy when exposed in base form.
- The player wins by reaching the spaceship.
